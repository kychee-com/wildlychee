# Kychon vs Wild Apricot

The most direct comparison. Wild Apricot is the incumbent for small associations and nonprofits. Kychon is built to serve the same market at a fraction of the cost.

## Pricing

| | Kychon | Wild Apricot |
|---|---|---|
| Base price | $5–20/mo (Run402 hosting tier) | $60–530/mo (per-contact tiers) |
| Per-member fees | None | Yes — price scales with contact count |
| Transaction fees | None (use your own Stripe) | 20% surcharge for non-affiliated payment gateway |
| AI customization | $9–29/mo (Kychon Pro, optional) | Not available |
| **Typical cost for 250-member org** | **~$14/mo** | **~$140/mo** |

## Where Wild Apricot wins

- **Payment processing** — Built-in recurring billing, invoicing, and payment collection. Kychon links to external Stripe/PayPal. For orgs that collect dues online, this is a meaningful gap.
- **Email marketing** — Wild Apricot includes email blasts, templates, and campaign tracking. Kychon has basic notifications only.
- **Maturity** — 15+ years in market. Thousands of edge cases handled. Kychon is new.
- **Reporting** — More granular financial and membership reports. Kychon has a dashboard but not deep analytics.
- **App marketplace** — Integrations with QuickBooks, Mailchimp, Zapier, etc.

## Where Kychon wins

- **Price** — 10x cheaper for a typical small org. No per-member pricing means cost doesn't scale with growth.
- **Modern design** — Clean, modern UI built in 2026. Wild Apricot's sites famously "look like 1995."
- **AI customization** — Ask an AI to add features, translate, retheme, restructure. Wild Apricot has no equivalent.
- **Inline editing** — Edit the live page directly. No separate admin backend with a different URL.
- **Own your data** — Self-hosted on Run402. Full database access. No vendor lock-in, no incomplete exports.
- **No transaction fees** — Use your own Stripe account. No surcharges.
- **Support** — Wild Apricot's support collapsed post-Personify acquisition (weeks for responses, no live support). Kychon is open-source with community support.
- **Performance** — ~15kB page load for members. Wild Apricot sites are heavy and slow.
- **i18n** — Built-in multi-language support. Wild Apricot has limited localization.

## Who should pick which

**Pick Wild Apricot if:** You need built-in payment processing and recurring billing today, you rely on email marketing campaigns, you need deep financial reporting, or you want a proven platform with 15 years of edge cases handled.

**Pick Kychon if:** You're price-sensitive ($14/mo vs $140/mo matters), you want a modern-looking site, you want AI-powered customization, you value owning your data, or you're frustrated with Wild Apricot's support and pricing trajectory.
