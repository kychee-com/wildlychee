# Kychon vs Mighty Networks

Mighty Networks targets community builders and course creators. It's the "community + courses" platform with native mobile apps. Different positioning than Kychon, but overlapping use cases for membership organizations.

## Pricing

| | Kychon | Mighty Networks |
|---|---|---|
| Base price | $5–20/mo | $49–360/mo |
| Transaction fees | None | 1–3% on paid memberships |
| Per-member fees | None | None |
| AI customization | $9–29/mo (optional) | Not available |
| **Typical cost** | **~$14/mo** | **~$49–99/mo + transaction fees** |

## Where Mighty Networks wins

- **Native mobile app** — Branded iOS/Android app on higher tiers. Kychon is web-only (responsive, but no native app).
- **Courses and learning** — Built-in course creation with progress tracking. Kychon doesn't have this.
- **Live streaming / live events** — Integrated video streaming. Kychon has no video features.
- **Community feed UX** — Mighty Networks is built around the feed. It's their core interaction model and it's polished.
- **Scale** — Handles large communities (10,000+ members) with mature infrastructure.

## Where Kychon wins

- **Price** — 3–7x cheaper. No transaction fees eating into membership revenue.
- **Membership management** — Kychon is built for organizations that manage members (tiers, approvals, renewals, directory). Mighty Networks is built for creators who build audiences.
- **AI customization** — Add features, translate, restructure via AI agent. Mighty Networks is what-you-see-is-what-you-get.
- **Own your data** — Full database access, self-hosted. Mighty Networks is SaaS with limited export.
- **Design flexibility** — Full CSS/HTML control. Mighty Networks has a fixed layout with theming options.
- **Support quality** — Mighty Networks has been criticized for AI-only support with no humans. Kychon is open-source.
- **No billing traps** — Mighty Networks has been flagged for auto-enrollment billing. Kychon is flat-rate, cancel anytime.
- **Lightweight** — ~15kB page load. Mighty Networks app is heavier.

## Who should pick which

**Pick Mighty Networks if:** You're a creator or educator building a community around courses, you need native mobile apps, you want live streaming, or your community is discussion/feed-centric rather than membership-management-centric.

**Pick Kychon if:** You're an organization that manages members (association, club, nonprofit, church), you want low cost with no transaction fees, you want full control over your site's design and data, or you need AI-powered customization.
