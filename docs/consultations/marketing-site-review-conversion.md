Big picture: your instinct is right.

Right now the homepage seems to **lead with a fight** when it should **lead with proof**. Your strongest assets are:

## Short answers

### 1) Hero: “Your community deserves better than Wild Apricot” — remove it?
**Yes, from the homepage.**

Why:
- It gives the competitor free mindshare.
- It narrows the page to people who already know Wild Apricot.
- It opens with negativity instead of aspiration.
- For orgs like churches, HOAs, associations, charities, the tone can feel a bit combative.

Use competitor language on:
- `/compare.html`
- better: `/compare/wild-apricot.html`, `/compare/circle.html`, `/compare/bettermode.html`
- paid ads / SEO landing pages for “X alternative”

### Better hero directions
I’d test one of these:

**Option A**
> **Own your member portal.**  
> Member directory, events, forum, announcements, and resources — on software you control, with typical hosting costs of $5–20/mo.

**Option B**
> **Membership software without per-member fees.**  
> Launch a beautiful community portal your organization actually owns.

**Option C**
> **A better home for your members.**  
> Everything your community needs, without the SaaS tax or lock-in.

And I would make the CTAs:

- **Primary:** Explore live demo
- **Secondary:** Get a custom build for $29
- **Tertiary text link:** View on GitHub

Also: if you have a live demo, **don’t point the main CTA to `#demo-placeholder`**. Point it directly to the live demo.

### 2) Problem section with Wild Apricot / Circle / Bettermode pricing — too aggressive?
**Yes, for the homepage.**  
Not because comparison is bad — comparison is good — but because it’s in the wrong place.

Homepage job:
- explain what it is
- show the product
- make visitors want it
- give them a clear next step

Comparison page job:
- capture high-intent “alternative to X” traffic
- justify switching
- answer migration objections

## Recommendation
Replace the current “Problem” section with something like:

### “Why organizations choose Kychon”
Three simple cards:
- **Predictable cost** — no per-member fees
- **Own your setup** — your data, branding, and account
- **Customize anything** — open source plus AI-assisted setup/customization

Then add one line underneath:
> Switching from Wild Apricot, Circle, or Bettermode? **See the full comparison.**

That’s the right balance.

Also: competitor pricing goes stale. On compare pages you can date-stamp it and cite sources.

### 4) Design: how to make it sexier / prettier / more memorable / more converting?
You already have a good base. **Instrument Serif + DM Sans** is a good pairing. Indigo/coral/cream is tasteful.

The issue is less “bad design” and more **not enough product-led art direction**.

## What I’d change

### A. Show the product in the hero
This is the biggest miss.

Put a large browser mockup of the Eagles portal right in the hero, with:
- subtle glow behind it
- soft shadow
- maybe 2–3 floating stat chips:
  - **25 members**
  - **10 events**
  - **No signup demo**

That instantly makes the page feel more premium and real.

### B. Replace card soup with screenshot-led sections
You currently have:
- problem cards
- feature cards
- AI cards
- showcase cards
- niche cards

That can feel like a long sequence of boxes.

Sexy SaaS pages usually alternate:
- headline + proof
- screenshot
- short benefit list
- visual demo
- pricing

Less “here are 14 cards”, more “here’s the product and why it matters.”

### C. Make the brand more ownable
Indigo + coral is nice, but a bit generic startup.

Because the brand is **Kychon**, I’d introduce a more lychee-specific accent:
- lychee pink / rose
- plum
- keep indigo as the trust color

Example direction:
- Primary: `#4338ca`
- Accent rose: `#f15b86`

Use rose/plum in gradients, highlight pills, hover glows.  
That would make the brand more memorable without becoming loud.

### 5) What’s missing from a CRO perspective?
The big missing pieces:

## A. A clearer primary conversion goal
Right now the page seems split between:
- demo clicks
- GitHub
- pricing
- niches

Pick a hierarchy.

My suggestion:
1. **Primary:** explore live demo
2. **Secondary:** buy/request Studio
3. **Tertiary:** GitHub

If paid conversions matter, GitHub should not be equal-weight with the main CTA.

---

## B. Product proof above the fold
You have a real demo. That should be visible immediately.

---

## C. “How it works”
You need a simple 3-step section:
1. Pick a starting template / niche
2. AI generates your initial portal
3. Launch on your domain and customize

This helps people understand the offer.

---

## D. FAQ / objection handling
You need to answer:
- Do I need technical skills?
- Is it really $5–20/mo?
- What’s included in Studio?
- Can you migrate from Wild Apricot?
- Can we use our own domain?
- Who owns the data?
- What does “AI-powered” actually do?

This will reduce bounce.

---

## E. Trust / legitimacy
Early-stage products often lack testimonials. That’s okay.

Use truthful trust signals:
- open source
- live demo
- real feature depth
- “fictional content, real software”
- GitHub link
- clear explanation of pricing model

Also, because your pricing is so low, you should add:
> **Why it’s so affordable**

Otherwise people may assume it must be missing something.
